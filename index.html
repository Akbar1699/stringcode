Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi



Mahatma Gandhi , often referred to as the Father of the Nation in India, was a prominent leader and advocate for nonviolent civil disobedience during the Indian independence movement against British rule.

About the Legend

☛ Early Life: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India..

☛ Education and Career: He studied law in London and practiced in South Africa, where he first experienced racial discrimination, sparking his activism.

☛ Nonviolent Resistance: Gandhi developed and popularized the concept of Satyagraha, a form of nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience, which he used effectively in the struggle for Indian independence

☛ Salt March: One of his most famous acts of civil disobedience was the Salt March in 1930, protesting against British salt monopoly in India.

☛ Philosophy of Ahimsa: Gandhi's philosophy was deeply rooted in the principle of ahimsa (nonviolence), which he applied not only politically but also in his personal life and relations.

☛ Role in Independence: He led nationwide campaigns for various social issues and was a key figure in negotiations with the British that led to India gaining independence in 1947.

☛ Legacy: Gandhi's legacy extends beyond India. His principles of nonviolence and civil disobedience influenced civil rights movements and leaders worldwide, including Martin Luther King Jr. in the United States.

☛ Assassination: Sadly, Gandhi was assassinated on January 30, 1948, by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist who opposed Gandhi's philosophy of tolerance towards Muslims.

Gandhi's life and teachings continue to inspire movements for social justice and nonviolent resistance globally, making him a revered figure in world history.
